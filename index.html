What is the difference between Sports Cars, Supercars, and Hypercars?



There are many categories of cars in the automotive world. None excite more than Sports Cars.

There is much debate about what categorizes a car as Sporty or Super and sometimes sparks heated conversations amongst Gearheads.

The Sports Car

Sports car have been referenced since the early 20th century. It was primarily used to define Two-seater roadsters (cars without fixed roofs).These cars provide enhanced or dynamic handling appart from your average car. Though the least expensive out of this list, they still provide plenty of fun and better driveability for everyday use out of the three. Examples can include:

  • Mazda Miata (MX-5)
  • Nissan 370z
  • BMW 3 Series Coupes

    • The Supercar

    Supercars can reach beyond the abilities of sportscars in terms of power, speed and technology. They have been defined by much higher horsepower and price points above sports cars and generally include more features in terms of infotainment system features and drive modes. Examples can include:

  • Chevy Corvette
  • Porsche 911 (base)
  • Acura NSX

    • The Hypercar

    We now evolved into the The Hypercar. The ultimate show and display of an automotive companies tenacity, engineering and innovation. This car is defined as being insanely fast, gorgeous design, and the latest in technology the company has to offer. Examples can include:

  • Bugatti Chiron
  • Aston Martin Valkyrie
  • Ferrari F90 Stradale
